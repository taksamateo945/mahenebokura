Top 20 Fastest Cars in the World

If you are a speed freak, you might be wondering what the fastest cars in the world are currently as I am wondering too.

Well, you are in luck, because I have compiled a list of the top 20 fastest cars that rock on roads today or will hit the roads soon.

These cars are not only fast, but also stunning, powerful, and expensive.

They are the ultimate machines for thrill-seekers and car enthusiasts alike.

Let’s take a look at these speed demons and see what makes them so special.

20. Rimac Nevera C Two

The Rimac Nevera C Two is the first fully electric car to make it to this list, and it is a beast.

Powered by a 120kWh battery pack, the Nevera uses four electric motors to produce a whopping 1,914 hp and 1,696 lb-ft of torque.

It can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 1.85 seconds, and reach a top speed of 258 mph.

The Nevera is also a technological marvel, with advanced features like facial recognition, adaptive aerodynamics, and torque vectoring.

The price tag for this electric hypercar is $2.4 million.

19. Bugatti Veyron 16.4 Super Sport

The Bugatti Veyron 16.4 Super Sport is a legend in the world of supercars.

It was the first car to break the 250 mph barrier in 2010, and it still holds the Guinness World Record for the fastest production car ever.

The Veyron Super Sport has a quad-turbocharged 8.0-liter W16 engine that delivers 1,200 hp and 1,106 lb-ft of torque.

It can sprint from 0 to 60 mph in 2.4 seconds, and reach a top speed of 267.85 mph.

The Veyron Super Sport is also one of the most expensive cars in the world, with a base price of $2.7 million.

18. Hennessey Venom GT

Hennessey Performance Engineering, a Texas-based tuner, built the monster of a car known as the Hennessey Venom GT.

It is based on a modified Lotus Exige chassis, and it has a twin-turbocharged 7.0-liter V8 engine that pumps out 1,244 hp and 1,155 lb-ft of torque.

The Venom GT can blast from 0 to 60 mph in 2.7 seconds, and reach a top speed of 270.49 mph.

Venom GT achieved this record in 2014 at the Kennedy Space Center in Florida, but Guinness did not officially recognize it because it was only achieved in one direction.

The Venom GT costs $1.2 million.

17. Koenigsegg Agera RS

The renowned supercar maker Koenigsegg created the Swedish masterpiece, the Koenigsegg Agera RS.

A twin-turbocharged 5.0-liter V8 engine powers it and produces 1,160 hp and 944 lb-ft of torque.

The Agera RS can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.6 seconds, and reach a top speed of 277.87 mph.

Agera RS achieved this feat in 2017 on a closed highway in Nevada, making it the fastest production car ever recorded on a public road.

The Agera RS is also extremely rare, with only 25 units made, each costing around $2.5 million.

16. Czinger 21C

The Czinger 21C is a futuristic hypercar that was designed and built by the California-based startup Czinger Vehicles.

It has a hybrid powertrain that consists of a twin-turbocharged 2.88-liter V8 engine and two electric motors that drive the front wheels.

The combined output is an astonishing 1,250 hp and 1,181 lb-ft of torque.

The Czinger 21C can rocket from 0 to 60 mph in just 1.9 seconds, and reach a top speed of 281 mph.

Czinger 21C is also a lightweight car, weighing only 2,645 pounds, thanks to its carbon fiber construction and innovative design.

The Czinger 21C will be limited to just 80 units, each priced at $1.7 million.

15. Bugatti Chiron Super Sport 300+

The Bugatti Chiron Super Sport 300+ is a special edition of the Bugatti Chiron that was created to celebrate the car’s record-breaking speed of 304.77 mph in 2019.

The Chiron Super Sport 300+ has a modified version of the quad-turbocharged 8.0-liter W16 engine that generates 1,578 hp and 1,180 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a more aerodynamic body, a longer tail, and reduced weight.

The Chiron Super Sport 300+ can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.3 seconds and has a limited top speed of 273.4 mph for safety reasons.

14. Bugatti Bolide

The Bugatti Bolide is a track-only concept car that showcases the extreme potential of the Bugatti W16 engine.

It has a weight-to-power ratio of 0.67 kg per hp, which means it weighs only 2,734 pounds and has a staggering 1,825 hp and 1,364 lb-ft of torque.

The Bolide can sprint from 0 to 60 mph in 2.17 seconds, and reach a top speed of over 310 mph.

Bugatti Bolide also has a sophisticated aerodynamics system, a lightweight carbon fiber monocoque, and a racing suspension.

Although not currently for sale, estimates suggest that the Bolide could cost around $10 million if it ever enters production.

13. Hennessey Venom F5

The Hennessey Venom F5 is the successor to the Hennessey Venom GT, and it aims to be the first road-legal car to break the 500 kph (310.7 mph) barrier.

The Venom F5 has a custom-built twin-turbocharged 6.6-liter V8 engine that produces an incredible 1,817 hp and 1,193 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a carbon fiber body, a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission, and a rear-wheel-drive system.

The Venom F5 can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in less than two seconds, and reach a top speed of over 310 mph.

They will limit the Venom F5 to only 24 units, with each unit costing $2.1 million.

12. Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut

The Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut is the ultimate version of the Koenigsegg Jesko, and it is designed to be the fastest Koenigsegg ever made.

The Jesko Absolut has a twin-turbocharged 5.0-liter V8 engine that delivers 1,600 hp and 1,106 lb-ft of torque on E85 fuel.

It also has a revolutionary nine-speed multi-clutch transmission, a lightweight carbon fiber chassis, and a sleek aerodynamic body that reduces drag to a minimum.

The Jesko Absolut can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.5 seconds, and reach a top speed of 330 mph, according to Koenigsegg’s simulations.

The Jesko Absolut will be produced in 125 units, each priced at around $3 million.

11. Devel Sixteen

The Devel Sixteen is a mysterious hypercar that claims to be the fastest car in the world for 2023.

The Devel Sixteen has a quad-turbocharged 12.3-liter V16 engine that allegedly produces 5,007 hp and 3,757 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a carbon fiber body, a six-speed sequential gearbox, and a rear-wheel-drive system.

The Devel Sixteen claims to be able to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 1.8 seconds and reach a top speed of 347 mph.

However, these numbers have not been verified by any independent source, and the Devel Sixteen’s production status is unclear.

The Devel Sixteen costs $2 million for the base model and $2.8 million for the top model.

10. SSC Tuatara

The SSC Tuatara is a hypercar that was developed by the American company SSC North America.

It has a twin-turbocharged 5.9-liter V8 engine that produces 1,750 hp and 1,280 lb-ft of torque on E85 fuel.

It also has a seven-speed automated manual transmission, a carbon fiber body, and a drag coefficient of 0.279.

The Tuatara can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.5 seconds, and reach a top speed of 331 mph.

In 2020, the Tuatara claimed to have achieved this speed on a public road in Nevada, but SSC later disputed and retracted the claim.

9. Koenigsegg One:1

The Koenigsegg One:1 is a hypercar that was created by Koenigsegg to celebrate its 20th anniversary in 2014.

It has a twin-turbocharged 5.0-liter V8 engine that delivers 1,360 hp and 1,011 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission, a carbon fiber chassis, and active aerodynamics.

The One:1 can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.5 seconds, and reach a top speed of over 270 mph.

They named the One:1 after its power-to-weight ratio of one horsepower per kilogram, making it one of the most extreme cars ever made.

The Bugatti Centodieci is a limited-edition hypercar that was unveiled by Bugatti in 2019 as a tribute to the iconic Bugatti EB110 from the 1990s.

It has a quad-turbocharged 8.0-liter W16 engine that generates 1,600 hp and 1,180 lb-ft of torque.

It also has an eight-speed dual-clutch transmission, a carbon fiber body, and a reduced weight of 4,356 pounds.

The Centodieci can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.4 seconds, and reach a top speed of 236 mph.

7. Pagani Huayra BC Roadster

The Pagani Huayra BC Roadster is a convertible version of the Pagani Huayra BC, which is itself a more powerful and lighter version of the Pagani Huayra.

It has a twin-turbocharged 6.0-liter V12 engine that produces 800 hp and 774 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a seven-speed single-clutch transmission, a carbon-titanium monocoque, and active aerodynamics.

The Huayra BC Roadster can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.8 seconds, and reach a top speed of 236 mph.

The Huayra BC Roadster will be limited to only 40 units, each costing $3.5 million.

6. Aston Martin Valkyrie

The Aston Martin Valkyrie is a hybrid hypercar that was developed by Aston Martin in collaboration with Red Bull Racing and other partners.

It has a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine that delivers 1,000 hp and 546 lb-ft of torque and an electric motor that adds 160 hp and 207 lb-ft of torque.

The total output is 1,160 hp and 664 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a seven-speed paddle-shift transmission, a carbon fiber structure, and an F1-inspired design.

The Valkyrie can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.5 seconds, and reach a top speed of 250 mph.

They will produce the Valkyrie in only 150 units, with each unit costing around $3 million.

5. Lamborghini Sian FKP 37

The Lamborghini Sian FKP 37 is a hybrid supercar that was unveiled by Lamborghini in 2019 as a tribute to the late Volkswagen Group chairman Ferdinand Piëch.

It has a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine that produces 785 hp and 531 lb-ft of torque, and a 48-volt electric motor that adds 34 hp and 28 lb-ft of torque.

The total output is 819 hp and 560 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission, a carbon fiber body, and a supercapacitor system that stores and releases energy faster than a battery.

The Sian FKP 37 can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.8 seconds, and reach a top speed of 220 mph.

They will limit the Sian FKP 37 to only 63 units, with each unit costing around $3.6 million.

4. Ferrari SF90 Stradale

The Ferrari SF90 Stradale is a plug-in hybrid supercar that was launched by Ferrari in 2019 as its flagship model.

It has a twin-turbocharged 4.0-liter V8 engine that delivers 769 hp and 590 lb-ft of torque, and three electric motors that add 217 hp and 195 lb-ft of torque.

The total output is 986 hp and 590 lb-ft of torque.

It also has an eight-speed dual-clutch transmission, a carbon fiber chassis, and an all-wheel-drive system.

The SF90 Stradale can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.5 seconds, and reach a top speed of 211 mph.

The SF90 Stradale costs around $625,000 for the base model, and $950,000 for the track-focused Assetto Fiorano version.

3. McLaren Speedtail

The McLaren Speedtail is a hybrid hypercar that was revealed by McLaren in 2018 as the successor to the legendary McLaren F1.

It has a twin-turbocharged 4.0-liter V8 engine that produces 746 hp and 590 lb-ft of torque

It also features an electric motor that provides an additional 308 hp and 256 lb-ft of torque.

The total output is 1,035 hp and 848 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission, a carbon fiber body, and a unique three-seat layout with the driver in the center.

The Speedtail can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 2.7 seconds, and reach a top speed of 250 mph.

The Speedtail will be produced in only 106 units, each costing around $2.25 million.

2. Pininfarina Battista

The Pininfarina Battista is an electric hypercar that was unveiled by the Italian design house Pininfarina in 2019 as its first-ever production car.

It has four electric motors that produce a combined output of 1,900 hp and 1,696 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a 120kWh battery pack that provides a range of over 300 miles on a single charge.

The Battista can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in less than two seconds, and reach a top speed of over 217 mph.

They will limit the Battista to only 150 units, with a cost of around $2.5 million each.

1. Tesla Roadster

Tesla announced the second generation of its original Roadster model as the electric supercar, the Tesla Roadster, in 2017

It has three electric motors that produce an estimated output of over 1,000 hp and over 7,000 lb-ft of torque.

It also has a 200kWh battery pack that provides a range of over 600 miles on a single charge.

The Roadster can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 1.9 seconds, and reach a top speed of over 250 mph.

Tesla plans to release the Roadster in late 2023 or early 2024, with a base price of $200,000.
