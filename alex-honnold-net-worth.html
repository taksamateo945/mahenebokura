Climbing Career

Alex Honnold started his climbing career in 2003 when he dropped out of college and began living in a van to travel around California and find the best climbing spots.

He remained relatively unknown until 2007 when he free-soloed two routes in Yosemite Valley: Astroman and The Rostrum.

He then gained more recognition and fame for his free solo ascents of big walls, such as Moonlight Buttress in Zion National Park, Half Dome in Yosemite National Park, and El Capitan in Yosemite National Park.

Honnold is the only person to have free soloed El Capitan, a feat that was documented in the Oscar-winning film Free Solo.

He also holds the record for the fastest ascent of the Yosemite triple crown, an 18-hour, 50-minute link-up of Mount Watkins, The Nose, and the Regular Northwest Face of Half Dome.

In addition to free soloing, Honnold has also climbed with partners and ropes on many challenging routes around the world.

In 2015, he won a Piolet d’Or for the Fitz Traverse in Patagonia with Tommy Caldwell.

He has also climbed in Morocco, China, Antarctica, and Greenland.
